Bilvashtakam

Sloka:Tridalam triguNaakaaram trinetram cha triyaayudham

trijanma paapasamhaaram eka Bilvam shivaarpaNam

Meaning: I offer the bilva patra to Shiva. This leaf embodies the three qualities of sattva, rajas and tamas. This leaf is like the three eyes, and the sun, moon and fire. It is like three weapons. It is the destroyer of sins committed in three earlier births. I perform pooja for Shiva with the bilva patra.

SlokaAkhanda bilvapatreNa poojite nandikeshware

shudhyantii sarva paapebhyo eka bilvam shivaarpaNam

Meaning: I offer the bilva patra to Shiva. I complete the pooja for Nandikeshwara by the bilva patra to him, and thus become free of sin

Sloka: Shaaligram shilaama ekaam vipraaNaam jaatu charpayet

somayadnya mahaapuNyam eka bilvam shivaarpaNam

Meaning:I offer the bilva patra to Shiva. This leaf is soft and free of blemish. It is complete in itself. It is like three branches. I perform pooja for Shiva with the bilva patra.

Sloka: DantikotisahasraNi vajape shataanicha

kotikanyaa mahaadaanam eka bilvam shivaarpaNam

Meaning:The offering of Bilva is greater in power than yagnas and sacrifices.

Sloka:Laxmyaa stnam unpanam mahadevasya cha priyam

bilva vruksham prayachchhaami eka bilvam shivaarpaNam

Meaning: The bilva tree was created by Goddess Lakshmi. Lord Shiva has great affection for the bilva tree. I perform pooja for Shiva with the bilva patra.

Sloka:Darshanam bilva vrukshasya sparshanam paapanaashanam

aghorapaapa samhaaram eka bilvam shivaarpaNam

Meaning Beholding the bilva and coming in contact with it destroys all sin. I perform pooja for Shiva with the bilva patra.

Sloka: Kashi kshetra nivasam cha kalabhairava darshanam

Prayaage maadhavam drushtvaa Ekabilvam shivaarpanam

Meaning:I offer one leaf of the bilva to Shiva, after being in the city of Kashi, beholding Kala Bhairava, and visiting the temple of Madhava

Sloka:Moolato brahma roopaaya madhyato Vishnu roopiNe

agrataha shiva roopaaya eka bilvam shivaarpaNam

Meaning:The lower part of bilva is Brahma, the middle is Vishnu and the upper is Shiva himself. I perform pooja for Shiva with the bilva patra.
